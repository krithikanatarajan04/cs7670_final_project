📋The Adoption Process: What to Expect

Our adoption process is designed to be thorough without being unnecessarily slow. Most applications are reviewed within 48 hours, and the typical adoption from application to pickup takes 3–7 days depending on scheduling and the animal's readiness.

  • Step 1: Browse available animals and read the full behavioral profile for any animal you're interested in.
  • Step 2: Submit an online application. Applications are specific to the animal, not general—please apply for the specific animal you want to adopt.
  • Step 3: A counselor reviews your application and may contact you with follow-up questions within 2 business days.
  • Step 4: Schedule a meet-and-greet at the shelter or foster home. Bring all household members, including resident pets if applicable.
  • Step 5: If approved, complete the adoption agreement and pay the adoption fee. Take your new family member home.
Adoption Requirements All adopters must be 18+. Renters must provide landlord approval documentation. Applications for dogs require a home check (virtual or in-person based on distance). All household members over 12 must be present at the meet-and-greet. We do not ship animals.

❓Frequently Asked Questions

What are the adoption fees?
Dogs: $150–$250 depending on age. Cats: $75–$125. Rabbits and small animals: $30–$60. All fees include spay/neuter, vaccinations, microchip, and a health certificate. Senior animals over 8 years: 50% reduction on all fees.
Can I adopt if I live in an apartment?
Yes. Many of our animals are well-suited to apartment living. We assess compatibility on the individual animal's needs and activity level, not on living space size alone. Please disclose apartment limitations in your application so we can guide you toward suitable matches.
What if the adoption doesn't work out?
We have a return policy with no judgment. We ask that you contact us before surrendering an animal back to us so we can prepare appropriately. Please do not post adopted animals on Craigslist or transfer ownership to another individual without contacting us first—this is a violation of the adoption agreement.
Do you work with out-of-state adopters?
We can work with adopters within a 200-mile radius who can travel to our facility for the meet-and-greet and pickup. We do not transport animals across state lines or conduct adoptions entirely remotely.
