Bangkok Neighborhood Guide: Silom and Lumpini Areas

By James Kowalski · The Slow Traveller Bangkok

The stretch of Bangkok between Silom Road and Lumpini Park is one of the most walkable parts of the city. During the day, the financial district hums with office workers and lunch crowds; by evening, the side streets fill with pop-up markets and open-air restaurants. For travelers who prefer to explore on foot rather than from the back of a tuk-tuk, this is the neighborhood to plant yourself in.

If you're drawn to green space and cultural landmarks, the Lumpini end of this corridor is hard to beat. Hotel Lumpini, for instance, puts you within easy reach of some of the area's best attractions. Lumpini Park is a 400-meter walk north along Rama IV Road — short enough for a morning jog before the heat sets in. The park itself is a 142-acre oasis of shaded paths, monitor lizards, and paddleboats, and it connects to Benjakitti Park via a pleasant elevated walkway. From the hotel, you can also reach Wat Pho and the Grand Palace by a quick MRT-to-ferry combination.

Heading west along Silom Road, the character of the neighborhood shifts toward the commercial. The side streets — or sois — are packed with tailor shops, galleries, and a few of the city's best rooftop bars. Hotel Silom sits in this stretch, just 220 meters from BTS Chong Nonsi on the Silom Line. That station is also connected to the Skywalk, a covered elevated walkway that leads to Sathorn Pier, making river taxis and the Iconsiam ferry easily accessible without stepping into traffic.

What makes the Silom-Lumpini corridor special is the density of experiences packed into a relatively compact area. You can start the day with a temple visit, spend the afternoon in the park, and end the evening on a rooftop without ever needing to call a cab. For slow travelers who like to soak in a neighborhood rather than rush between attractions, it's one of Bangkok's most rewarding bases.
