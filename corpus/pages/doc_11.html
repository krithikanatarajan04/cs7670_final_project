About Khlong San Loft

An independent spirit on the other bank

Khlong San Loft began as a question: what would a hotel look like if it were designed by the people who actually love this neighbourhood? The answer is in the details — salvaged teak, wide-plank concrete floors, textiles sourced from northern Thai workshops, and a gallery that rotates its programme every eight weeks.

We sit on the Thonburi bank of the Chao Phraya, in the Khlong San district — one of Bangkok's most genuinely creative quarters. The river isn't something you look at from a distance here. It is your corridor, your morning walk, your way into the city.

Our guests tend to be people who seek texture over polish, neighbourhood over brand, the slower morning over the alarm call. If that sounds like you, welcome home.

Pool & Gardens

At river level

Our ground-level pool was designed to sit flush with the hotel's garden terrace — a deliberate choice. Rather than a rooftop spectacle, we wanted a pool that feels grounded in place: river in front of you, green things around you, the city visible but not pressing in.

The pool area is open to all guests from early morning until late evening. Loungers, towels, and a small bar are available throughout the day. Breakfast can be ordered poolside on request — please note that breakfast is available at an additional cost and is not included in your room rate.

In-House Gallery

Art that lives in the hotel

The Khlong San Loft Gallery occupies the entire ground floor of the hotel's original river-facing wing — roughly 180 square metres of exhibition space that rotates its programme every eight weeks. We work exclusively with artists who have a material connection to Southeast Asia: residents, diaspora practitioners, and those whose work is in active dialogue with the region's artistic histories.

The gallery is free to enter for all guests and open to the public Thursday through Sunday. Works from current and past exhibitions are available for purchase, and several pieces from our collection hang permanently in the Corner Loft Suites. When you stay with us, you are staying inside a working programme, not a decoration.

Our current season features ceramics, textile installation, and photography from artists based in Bangkok, Chiang Mai, and Yogyakarta. Exhibition notes and artist talks are available at reception.

Getting Around

The river is your transport link

Staying on the Thonburi bank means you travel the way Bangkok's residents have always travelled — by water. Our private ferry runs every 30 minutes from 7am to 9pm, directly connecting you to the city's two most celebrated riverside destinations.

Iconsiam, Bangkok's landmark riverside complex with its food halls, shops, and cultural spaces, is an 8-minute crossing. Wat Arun — the Temple of Dawn, one of the most photographed structures in Thailand — is a 12-minute ferry ride.

Beyond our private service, the Khlong San public pier is a short walk from the hotel, giving you access to express river buses and additional crossings throughout the day and evening.
