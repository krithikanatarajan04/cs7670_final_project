The Best Laptop Sleeves in 2024: 7 Options Tested and Ranked

We carried these sleeves for 60 days combined, dropped them, stuffed them in backpacks, and got them rained on. Here's what survived and what didn't.
✍️ Marco Teller 📅 Oct 3, 2024 ⏱️ 12 min read 🖥️ Tested on: 13" MacBook Air M3, 15" MBP, Dell XPS 15

Laptop sleeves exist in a strange product category: the price range runs from $9 to $250, the reviews on major retailers are mostly garbage, and the difference between a bad sleeve and a good one only becomes apparent when your laptop gets scratched by the zipper hardware you didn't examine before buying.

This comparison started because I damaged a laptop on a previous sleeve's exposed zipper pull. That was €900 worth of lesson. These seven sleeves were purchased at retail and tested under real commuting conditions over two months, including a very unfortunate encounter with a burst water bottle in a backpack. Results below.

Top Picks in Detail

Best Overall
Tomtoc 360° Protective Sleeve
$39

The Tomtoc earns its position through a genuinely clever design decision: the zipper path is routed along the back of the sleeve, with a protective flap that keeps the zipper hardware away from the laptop screen at all times. After two months of daily use, there isn't a mark on the laptop it's protecting. The ballistic nylon exterior handled the water bottle incident with minimal internal moisture intrusion.

Best Value
Inateck 13-inch Sleeve
$18

At $18, the Inateck should not perform as well as it does. The felt exterior is legitimately attractive and doesn't show light scuffs. Internal neoprene provides solid cushioning for everyday use. The single major drawback is water resistance: felt does not repel moisture effectively, and this sleeve would not survive the water bottle test the Tomtoc handled. For commuters who aren't carrying the sleeve exposed in wet conditions, it's a legitimate recommendation.

Avoid
AmazonBasics Neoprene Sleeve
$11

The zipper hardware sits directly against the laptop surface when closed. In two weeks of testing, light scratches appeared on the case of an aluminum laptop. The neoprene is also notably thinner than any other sleeve in this comparison. There is no version of this product that represents a good value relative to the Inateck at $7 more.

VERDICT: Do not buy this. Spend $7 more on the Inateck.

What to Look For: Buying Criteria

Before buying any sleeve, check four things: (1) zipper hardware placement relative to where the laptop screen will rest, (2) interior material—rough or hard surfaces will scratch aluminum finishes over time, (3) the exact interior dimensions versus your laptop's actual dimensions, and (4) whether the sleeve is intended to be carried standalone or inside a backpack, because optimal padding differs between the two use cases.
