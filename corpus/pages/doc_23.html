Curator Talk
The Hidden Language of Maps: Cartography as Power, 1600–1900
With Dr. Helena Marsh, Curator of Historical Documents
TicketedMembers $5 Off

A deep examination of how the maps of the early modern period were not neutral representations of geography—they were political documents, tools of empire, and vehicles of cultural narrative. Dr. Marsh will draw from the museum's Alderton Collection of 200+ historical maps, many of which have never been displayed publicly, to trace how cartographic choices embedded power relationships into the visual record of the world.

Followed by a 30-minute Q&A and a guided viewing of the collection highlights in Gallery 4. Wine and light refreshments served for members beginning at 6:30 PM.

Fossil Casting Studio: Make Your Own Trilobite
Hands-on workshop for ages 6–12
Ages 6–12Free with Admission

Children work alongside museum educators to create plaster casts of real fossil molds, learning about the Paleozoic era and how fossils form in the process. Each child takes home their cast at the end of the session. Materials and aprons provided—please dress for mess.

Guided Exhibition Tour
Inside "Atmosphere": A Guided Tour of the New Climate Art Exhibition
Led by exhibition co-curator James Nwosu
Free with AdmissionPriority Seating for Members

James Nwosu, who co-curated the newly opened "Atmosphere" exhibition exploring how contemporary artists are engaging with climate change and ecological transformation, leads an intimate 60-minute walkthrough of the show. Nwosu will speak about the acquisition process, the artists' relationships to their subjects, and the specific installation choices made in the museum's light court.

"Atmosphere" features 23 works by artists from 14 countries, including large-scale installations, photography, mixed media, and video art. The exhibition runs through February 16, 2025.

Film Screening
The Mushroom Speaks: A Documentary on Mycelium and Forest Intelligence
Followed by a discussion with mycologist Dr. Priya Suresh
Ticketed

A 78-minute documentary examining the growing body of research on mycorrhizal networks—the underground fungal systems that connect trees and plants across forest ecosystems. The film features research from multiple institutions and follows three scientists across field seasons in temperate rainforests, old-growth boreal forests, and urban woodlands. Following the screening, Dr. Priya Suresh joins for a moderated conversation about the current state of the science and its implications for conservation policy.
