Baratza Encore ESP vs. Comandante C40: Which Should You Actually Buy?

A rigorous, side-by-side comparison for serious home brewers
AUTHOR: J. Wren  |  OCT 8, 2024  |  16 min read  |  2 grinders tested over 6 weeks

There are two types of coffee grinder buyers: people who want to stop thinking about this and buy something reliable, and people who are already thinking too much about it and need empirical evidence to make peace with a decision. This review serves both, but it leans toward the latter—because this is Grind Report, and the whole premise is that grinding details matter.

We pulled both grinders from our own shelves (purchased at retail, because that's the policy), ground through 3 kilograms of coffee on each, and ran comparative extractions across espresso, pour-over, and French press. Here's what actually happened.

The Encore ESP is the electric entry-level grinder that Baratza has quietly been refining for years. The ESP suffix signals the addition of a stepped adjustment ring for espresso that helps dial in finer settings with more repeatability. In our testing, consistency at filter ranges was excellent—sieve analysis showed a tight distribution that outperformed grinders costing $80–$100 more. At espresso ranges, the stepped ring helps, but the 40-step macro adjustment still means finding the exact shot you want requires patience and a willingness to waste some coffee during dial-in.

VERDICT: The best electric grinder under $250. Practical for filter brewing daily and capable at espresso with patience. Buy this if you want one machine that works reliably and quietly enough not to wake a partner at 6am (mostly).

The Comandante C40 is a hand grinder that produces particle distributions rivaling electric grinders costing $500 or more. The Nitro Blade steel is genuinely different from standard stainless—harder, sharper, more resistant to wear at high temperatures. Zero retention means what goes in comes out, with no stale ghost grinds from previous sessions. The tradeoff is 90–120 seconds of hand-cranking for a full pour-over dose, which is either meditative or maddening depending on your morning disposition. Not compatible with espresso: the grind is too coarse at minimum setting for most machines.

VERDICT: The best manual grinder available, period. If you make filter coffee and want the cleanest cup possible, this is the answer. If you make espresso or value speed, look elsewhere.

Which Should You Buy?

This comparison is somewhat unfair by design—these grinders serve genuinely different purposes, and the question of which is "better" can only be answered by asking what your coffee life actually looks like.

  • If you make espresso at home: Baratza Encore ESP, without hesitation. The Comandante cannot reach espresso-fine grind settings.
  • If you make pour-over or filter only and want the absolute best cup: Comandante C40. The grind quality is demonstrably superior.
  • If you're always running late in the morning: Baratza Encore ESP. 8 seconds versus 2 minutes is a real lifestyle consideration.
  • If you travel and want café-quality coffee anywhere: Comandante C40. It's made for this.
