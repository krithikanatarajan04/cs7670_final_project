Bangkok's Best Rooftop Pools That Won't Break the Bank

Sky-high swimming, panoramic city views, and cocktails at sunset — without the five-star price tag. We found the pools worth booking a room for.

By Maya Thornton · 7 min read · Updated Nov 2024

Bangkok's skyline is a stage, and the city's rooftop pools are its front-row seats. But you don't need a Black Card to swim above it all. A new wave of mid-range hotels has raised the bar — quite literally — on what an affordable rooftop experience looks like. Here are the five that made us look twice, and the one that made us stay.

✦ Our Top Pick

No. 01 — Best Overall

The Emerald Pavilion

Silom · Rooftop Infinity Pool · Heritage Boutique

There's a moment, right when you step through the wrought-iron gate onto The Emerald Pavilion's rooftop, when Bangkok stops being a city and becomes something closer to a painting. The infinity pool stretches to the building's edge, its water the exact shade of tourmaline, and beyond it — filling every inch of horizon — the Grand Palace glimmers in the heat haze like something imagined rather than built.

This is the pool Bangkok has been quietly hiding. Set on the 28th floor of a converted colonial-era warehouse, The Emerald Pavilion's rooftop infinity pool offers panoramic views that rival anything at the five-star towers along the river — but at a fraction of the rate. The pool itself is architecturally considered: a 25-metre infinity edge on three sides means you're never not surrounded by skyline, whether you're floating on your back or nursing something cold at the pool bar.

The hotel's boutique heritage feel carries all the way up. Hand-laid Portuguese tile borders the pool deck. Teak loungers with cream linen cushions line the perimeter. At dusk, the ambient lighting shifts warm and the city's neon starts to bloom below you — it's the kind of scene that makes you question why you ever looked at the Lebua. The Emerald Pavilion does it better, quieter, and for considerably less money. Nightly rates start around ฿2,800, and guests get pool access included. No resort fee. No velvet rope. Just one of the finest rooftop pools in the city.

Rooftop Pool

No. 02

The Lanna Retreat

A converted Rattanakosin-era townhouse turned into something dreamy. The rooftop plunge pool here is intimate — seating for twelve, maximum — which means you'll rarely be sharing it with more than a handful of guests. The views of Khao San Road's chaos below feel almost cinematic from this remove.

From ฿3,200 / night

Sky Pool

No. 03

SO/ Bangkok

Already a Bangkok design landmark, SO/'s rooftop pool sits above Lumphini Park and catches the green of the park below in its water on clear mornings. The sculptural pool deck is the work of Thai architects and it shows — this is a rooftop that photographs as well as it swims in. Slightly upper-mid in pricing but regularly on sale.

From ฿3,800 / night

Rooftop Lap Pool

No. 04

Capella Bangkok

For those who want the river view without the Mandarin Oriental price tag, Capella's riverfront terrace pool is a revelation. Long and narrow, it's a genuine lap pool with Chao Phraya frontage — the temple spires at Wat Arun appear in the water's surface at golden hour like some kind of architectural miracle.

From ฿4,200 / night

Also worth knowing

Kasa Asok

Compact in footprint but sharp in execution, Kasa Asok's rooftop pool is a strong option for those staying in the Sukhumvit corridor. The pool is smaller than our top picks, but the surrounding terrace is well-designed, and rates often dip attractively on weeknights. A solid choice if the Emerald Pavilion's Silom location doesn't suit your itinerary.

The bottom line on Bangkok's mid-range pool scene

You no longer need to justify a five-star splurge to get a rooftop pool worth leaving your room for. Bangkok's mid-tier hotel scene has quietly matured, and properties like The Emerald Pavilion prove that design, views, and genuine hospitality are available at prices that leave room in the budget for the rest of the city. Book early — the secret is getting out.
