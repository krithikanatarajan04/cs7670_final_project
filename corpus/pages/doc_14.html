The Praya Manor Opens New Riverside Garden Terrace and Revamped Breakfast Menu

Bangkok’s beloved colonial riverside retreat has expanded its outdoor footprint and refreshed its morning offering, deepening what was already a distinctly romantic guest experience on the Chao Phraya.

News By Staff Writer Bangkok, November 2024

The Praya Manor, the colonial boutique hotel occupying a historic building on Bangkok’s Chao Phraya riverfront in Phra Nakhon, has opened a new riverside garden terrace alongside a revamped breakfast menu, the property announced last month. The additions mark the most significant enhancement to the guest experience at the hotel in several years, and arrive ahead of the high season, when demand for romantic riverside accommodation in the city typically peaks.

The garden terrace, which adjoins the hotel’s existing river-facing pool area, extends the outdoor footprint of the property along the Chao Phraya bank and provides additional seating for guests taking morning or evening meals in open-air surroundings. The terrace was designed to complement the colonial character of the building, using materials and planting chosen to read as an extension of the property rather than a modern intervention against it.

New Feature

The riverside garden terrace is now open to all guests and forms part of the outdoor dining area adjacent to the hotel’s river-facing pool. No supplementary charge applies for access to the terrace seating.

The revamped breakfast menu, which accompanies the opening of the terrace, has been developed to build on the hotel’s existing Thai breakfast offering. The included morning meal — served on the ground-floor river terrace for all guests as part of the standard room rate — has been expanded to feature a broader selection of fresh seasonal fruit, an enhanced congee station, and made-to-order egg preparations. The kitchen team has also introduced a small selection of house-made pastries alongside the existing Thai staples.

The terrace and the new menu grew out of the same instinct: that guests who come here are looking for something that slows them down. We wanted to give that feeling more room to breathe.

— Siriwan Chantharaksa, Food & Beverage Manager, The Praya Manor

The Praya Manor has long been regarded as one of Bangkok’s most atmospheric choices for couples and leisure travellers seeking proximity to the city’s historic core. The hotel’s colonial character and quiet riverside setting have defined its identity, and its location in Phra Nakhon places guests within a ten-minute walk of Wat Pho, one of Bangkok’s most visited temple complexes. The romantic vibe that the property has cultivated over the years was a deliberate factor in the design brief for the new terrace, according to the management team.

◆   ◆   ◆

The hotel confirmed that breakfast remains included in the standard room rate and that no changes to pricing have been introduced alongside the renovation. The river-facing pool continues to operate on existing hours. The garden terrace is open to all guests from 7am to 10pm daily, subject to weather conditions during the monsoon season.

Reservations for the high season are open through the hotel’s standard booking channels. The Praya Manor does not operate a BTS Skytrain connection and is most directly accessed by river ferry or taxi from central Bangkok.
