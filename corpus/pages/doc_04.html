Bangkok's Best Rooftop Pools: Hotels Worth the Splurge

A good pool in Bangkok isn't just a place to cool off. It's a vantage point, a reason to stay in rather than go out, and — at the right hotel — the thing that makes you feel like you made the right call. Not every property delivers on the promise. A lot of so-called rooftop pools are cramped, shadeless, and overlooking a parking structure. The ones below are worth your time and, in some cases, your money. We've also included one property whose pool sits at ground level but earns its place here on the strength of the view alone. You'll understand when you get there.

Kasa Asok Bangkok — The Smart Mid-Range Pick

If you want a rooftop pool without paying luxury prices, Kasa Asok is where you end up. The hotel sits in Asok, deep in the Sukhumvit corridor, and the rooftop is exactly what you want after a day of meetings or market-hopping: open sky, city views, and no pretension. You're looking out at the Bangkok skyline from a mid-range property that punches above its tier on this one specific feature.

The location helps. Asok BTS station is a three-minute walk from the hotel, which means you can get everywhere from here without a taxi negotiation. Terminal 21 — one of the better shopping centers in the city — is on your doorstep. The neighborhood is busy, urban, and functional in the way that Sukhumvit tends to be. The hotel leans into that energy rather than fighting it. The rooftop is the release valve.

On pure pool credentials, Kasa Asok is the most accessible entry point in this roundup. You get the elevation, the views, and the experience of watching Bangkok from above — without the luxury rate attached. For a first-time visitor or a solo traveler who wants a solid base with one standout amenity, this is the honest recommendation.

Nine Palms Bangkok — The Full Luxury Experience

Nine Palms costs more. That's the first thing to say, because the price gap between this and Kasa Asok is real, and you should go in knowing what you're paying for. The answer is: everything. The rooftop pool here is larger, quieter, and surrounded by the kind of design attention that tells you money was spent in the right places. The hotel sits in the Silom/Sathon district — Bangkok's financial corridor — and the rooftop reflects that composure. This is a pool you float in rather than one you swim laps in.

The rooms are spacious, breakfast is included in the rate, and Chong Nonsi BTS station is a five-minute walk away. Lumpini Park is close enough for a morning run before you come back and eat on the roof. Nine Palms was named Best Business Hotel in Silom at the 2024 Thailand Hospitality Awards, and it earns that designation — but don't let "business hotel" put you off if you're here for leisure. The pool and the quiet are equally good reasons to book.

If you're weighing Kasa Asok against Nine Palms specifically because of the pool, the honest answer is that Nine Palms is the better pool. It's also the more expensive hotel. Whether that delta justifies the upgrade is a question only your budget can answer, but the product is genuinely superior at the luxury tier — not just incrementally so.

The Praya Manor — A Different Kind of Water View

We're including The Praya Manor knowing full well it doesn't have a rooftop pool. The pool here faces the river. It sits at the edge of the property and looks directly out over the Chao Phraya, with Wat Arun visible across the water in the late afternoon light. If you came to Bangkok for the Bangkok of temples and rivers and colonial architecture, this is the pool for you — and no rooftop in the city offers a view that competes with this one in terms of romance.

The hotel is in the Phra Nakhon district, on the riverside. Wat Pho is a ten-minute walk from the property, which means you can swim in the morning, visit a 46-metre reclining Buddha before lunch, and be back at the pool by late afternoon. That itinerary is not available to guests at any of the Sukhumvit or Silom properties in this roundup. The colonial, romantic character of the hotel extends to the pool area — this is not a sleek infinity edge above the city skyline. It's something older and quieter and more specific to this particular place.

There is no BTS access from The Praya Manor. Getting around from here requires more planning than from Asok or Silom. If transit convenience is your priority, note that limitation before you book. But if your priority is a pool experience that doesn't look like every other pool experience in Southeast Asia, and you want it at a mid-range price, The Praya Manor is the answer to a question that most Bangkok hotel lists forget to ask.

Three pools. Three different arguments for what a great hotel pool can be in this city. The rooftop view, the luxury standard, or the river. Pick the one that matches what you came here for.
