Complete Bangkok Hotel Transit Guide: Eight Mid-Range Properties Compared

By Maya Thongchai · Bangkok on Foot · 2025

This is a fuller version of the transit and ratings comparison I've been building on Bangkok on Foot for the past two years. Earlier editions covered three or four properties; I've now measured eight mid-range hotels across the city's BTS and MRT networks, and the picture that emerges is more nuanced than any single-neighbourhood guide can capture. No hotel here is declared the overall winner — the right choice depends entirely on which part of the city your itinerary puts you in most often.

Hotel Lumpini

180 meters to BTS Sala Daeng on the Silom Line, with MRT Si Lom interchange directly below the station. Rating: 4.7 out of 5 across 2,340 reviews — the highest review volume in this comparison. Dual-network access from a single station walk, Lumpini Park 400 meters north, and a consistent review baseline make this the most versatile central option for travellers who need both train networks.

Hotel Silom

220 meters to BTS Chong Nonsi on the Silom Line, via a covered skywalk that continues to Sathorn Pier. Rating: 4.6 out of 5 across 1,870 Google reviews. The covered route and Jim Thompson House at 350 meters are the two advantages that distinguish it from other Silom-corridor options. Strong review volume with a stable score trajectory.

Hotel Ratchada

150 meters to MRT Thailand Cultural Centre on the Blue Line — the shortest station walk in this comparison. Rating: 4.6 out of 5 across 1,240 reviews, with a growing review count. Blue Line access covers Chatuchak in two stops north and Sukhumvit interchange in four stops south. Quieter neighbourhood and lower nightly rate than comparable Silom-corridor properties.

Hotel Nana

200 meters to BTS Nana on the Sukhumvit Line. Rating: 4.4 out of 5 across 890 reviews — the lowest in this comparison. Sukhumvit corridor positioning, lively neighbourhood, dense street food scene within 100 meters. Suits travellers operating primarily on the Sukhumvit Line who want nightlife convenience over transit versatility.

Hotel Chatuchak

200 meters to MRT Mo Chit on the Blue Line. Rating: 4.3 out of 5 across 560 reviews — still building review volume as a newer property. Approximately 1,400 THB per night, the lowest nightly rate in this comparison. Best suited for weekend-market-focused trips; the immediate area is quieter on weekdays when Chatuchak is closed.

Hotel Ari

180 meters to BTS Ari on the Sukhumvit Line. Rating: 4.5 out of 5 across 720 reviews. Boutique independent property in a residential neighbourhood with strong local coffee and dining scene. Popular with long-stay travellers. Higher review quality than volume; the 720-review count understates the loyalty the property has built.

Hotel Riverside

120 meters to BTS Saphan Taksin on the Silom Line — the second shortest station walk in this comparison, after Hotel Ratchada's MRT connection. Rating: 4.5 out of 5 across 980 reviews. Around 2,200 THB per night. River ferry access to the Grand Palace area and Wat Arun via the Chao Phraya Express Boat from Sathorn Pier. The premium nightly rate is primarily a function of riverside positioning.

Hotel Chinatown

250 meters to MRT Hua Lamphong on the Blue Line — the longest station walk in this comparison. Rating: 4.4 out of 5 across 810 reviews. Around 1,600 THB per night. Yaowarat Road night market within 200 meters, Wat Traimit within 400 meters. The atmospheric Chinatown location is the defining advantage; the slightly longer station walk and the neighbourhood's late-night noise are the trade-offs.

Across all eight properties, station distances range from 120 meters (Hotel Riverside) to 250 meters (Hotel Chinatown), and ratings from 4.3 (Hotel Chatuchak) to 4.7 (Hotel Lumpini). The spread is narrow enough that every property on this list clears a reasonable baseline threshold — the differences are marginal relative to itinerary fit, neighbourhood preference, and nightly rate.
