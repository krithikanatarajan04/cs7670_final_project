Riverside and Silom Hotels: Transit Access and Value Compared

By Yuki Nakamura · Bangkok River and Rail · 2025

Bangkok's river and rail systems intersect most usefully at the city's southern end, where the Chao Phraya ferry network meets the BTS Skytrain at Saphan Taksin and Sathorn Pier. For travellers whose itineraries revolve around the riverside temples, the night markets along the water, and the Silom corridor's restaurants and cultural sites, the choice of hotel base in this southern zone has become more interesting as properties closer to the Saphan Taksin station have improved. Here's how the current options compare when you weigh transit access against guest satisfaction and nightly rate.

Hotel Riverside

Hotel Riverside sits 120 meters from BTS Saphan Taksin on the Silom Line — the shortest station walk of the three properties covered here — at approximately 2,200 THB per night, positioning it at the upper end of the mid-range tier. The 4.5 out of 5 rating across 980 Google reviews reflects a property that has found a consistent guest base: mostly couples, according to the review text, who cite the river access and the evening atmosphere along the embankment as the primary draws. The practical advantage over other Silom-corridor properties is the ferry connection: Saphan Taksin is the main pier for the Chao Phraya Express Boat, which runs northbound to the Grand Palace pier (Tha Chang) and Wat Arun's ferry crossing at Tha Tien. For guests planning to visit those sites, the river ferry is a genuinely pleasant alternative to rail travel — slower, but scenic, and less crowded on weekday mornings. The nightly rate premium over Hotel Silom is real; whether it's justified depends on how much of the trip revolves around the riverside.

Hotel Silom

At 220 meters from BTS Chong Nonsi, Hotel Silom offers slightly less direct station access than Hotel Riverside but covers the distance via a skywalk that connects to the Sathorn Pier elevated walkway — giving it an indirect river connection of its own for travellers willing to walk an extra few minutes. The 4.6 out of 5 across 1,870 Google reviews makes it the strongest-rated property by review volume in this comparison. The established review base and the lower nightly rate relative to Hotel Riverside make it a strong default for travellers who want Silom-corridor positioning without paying for the premium riverside location. Jim Thompson House at 350 meters adds a cultural attraction that doesn't require any transit cost.

Hotel Lumpini

Hotel Lumpini, at 180 meters from BTS Sala Daeng, anchors the northern end of this corridor. The Sala Daeng station's dual-network function — BTS Silom Line plus MRT Si Lom interchange below — makes it the most versatile transit node in the area, and the 4.7 rating across 2,340 reviews gives it the deepest review base of the three. Lumpini Park's 400-meter walk north is the neighbourhood differentiator; guests who want green space within walking range and don't need the river at their doorstep tend to gravitate here. The nightly rate sits between Hotel Riverside's premium and Hotel Silom's mid-range position.

For river-focused itineraries, Hotel Riverside's 120-meter BTS walk and direct ferry access represent genuine value despite the rate premium. For Silom corridor versatility and review confidence, Hotel Silom. For dual-network access and park proximity, Hotel Lumpini.
