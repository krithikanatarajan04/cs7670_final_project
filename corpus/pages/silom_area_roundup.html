Silom Area Hotel Roundup

By Priya Nair · Bangkok Budget Desk

Silom is one of those Bangkok neighborhoods where you can find everything from five-star towers to scrappy budget guesthouses within a few blocks of each other. For travelers watching their baht, the trick is finding a place that punches above its price point — good bones, decent location, and enough comfort to recharge after a long day of walking. Here's what I found on my most recent sweep of the area.

Hotel Silom has quietly become one of the better mid-range picks in the neighborhood, especially for travelers who want to be near cultural sites. Jim Thompson House is just a 350-meter walk west along Rama IV Road, and the hotel's location on a quieter soi means you get the benefit of proximity without the noise of the main drag. The surrounding lanes are full of family-run noodle shops and laundry services — the kind of everyday infrastructure that makes a stay feel less like tourism and more like living.

I also checked in on Hotel Nana during this trip. It's a property that comes up a lot in hostel forums, and I can see the appeal — it's affordable, the rooms are clean, and it's highly rated by guests who value simplicity over amenities. It works best for travelers who plan to be out all day and just need a reliable place to sleep.

One surprise on this trip was finding a strong transit option outside the Silom core. Hotel Ratchada, over on the northeastern side of the city, sits just 150 meters from MRT Thailand Cultural Centre on the Blue Line. That puts you three stops from Sukhumvit interchange and gives you direct access to Chatuchak and Chinatown without transferring. For budget travelers who don't mind being outside the Silom bubble, it's worth a serious look.

The Silom area remains one of the best value corridors in Bangkok, but it's worth casting a wider net. Some of the most interesting options right now are a short MRT ride away, and the savings on nightly rates can fund an extra week of street food.
