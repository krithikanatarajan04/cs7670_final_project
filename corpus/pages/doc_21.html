The Complete Apartment Cleaning Checklist (Daily, Weekly & Monthly)

Stop cleaning reactively. This system builds a routine that keeps your apartment consistently clean with less than two hours of effort a week.
By Maeve Callahan  •  Published Oct 5, 2024  •  Updated Oct 11, 2024  •  11 min read

The difference between a clean apartment and a dirty one isn't usually time—it's habits and sequence. Most people clean too much at once and too infrequently, which makes each session feel like an overwhelming event rather than a normal part of life. This guide builds a three-tier system: daily tasks that take minutes, weekly tasks that take under an hour, and monthly tasks that address the things no one wants to think about until they become embarrassing.

Use the checklists below exactly as-is, or adapt them to your specific apartment layout. The times listed are realistic estimates based on a 700–1000 sq ft one or two-bedroom apartment. Smaller studio dwellers can trim about 30% off the totals.

The Daily Routine (10–15 minutes total)

The goal of the daily routine is containment—keeping entropy from compounding. Doing these tasks prevents the Friday-night spiral where you spend three hours undoing a week of accumulated disorder before anyone comes over.

3 minQuick Dishes

Empty any "drop zones" (entry table, kitchen island, bed)

The Weekly Deep(er) Clean (45–60 minutes)

Once a week, you go slightly beyond the surface. This is where you vacuum, mop, clean the toilet, and do the tasks that don't need to happen every day but that build up noticeably if skipped more than seven days.

💡 Pro TipDo your weekly clean in the same order every time. Starting top-to-bottom (dusting before vacuuming, vacuuming before mopping) means you're never re-cleaning what you've already done. Pick a day and make it non-negotiable—Saturday morning before coffee, or Sunday evening before the week starts.

Vacuum all floors (rugs, carpet, and hard floors for debris)

Scrub bathroom sink and tub/shower
Wipe down all mirrors and glass surfaces

The Monthly Tasks (30–45 minutes extra)

Monthly tasks address the things that don't need frequent attention but become genuinely gross if never addressed. This list is shorter than most people expect, because the daily and weekly routines handle most ongoing maintenance already.

Monthly ChecklistOnce Per Month
Clean inside the refrigerator (remove everything, wipe shelves, check for expired items)
Clean the oven and vent hood filter
Wash all bedding including pillows and duvet cover
Wipe down baseboards and window sillsThese collect dust in a way that's invisible until suddenly it isn't
Clean behind and under large appliances (fridge, stove, washer if applicable)
Descale the coffee maker and kettle
Wipe down interior of all cabinets and drawers in kitchen
